# Video Script (10-12 Minutes)

## Opening 
Good morning/afternoon.
In this video, I am presenting Assignment 01, where I compare and explain six software
development models: Waterfall, Agile, Scrum, Kanban, DevOps, and the Spiral Model.

## Part 1: Quick Overview of 6 Models 
- Waterfall is a sequential model with fixed stages and strong documentation.
- Agile is iterative and adapts quickly to changing requirements.
- Scrum is an Agile framework using sprints, defined roles, and regular ceremonies.
- Kanban visualizes work and improves flow by limiting work in progress.
- DevOps combines development and operations with automation and CI/CD.
- Spiral is a risk-driven iterative model that emphasizes risk analysis each cycle.

## Part 2: Agile vs Waterfall 
- Waterfall plans most things early; Agile plans continuously.
- Waterfall works best when requirements are stable; Agile is better when requirements change.
- Customer feedback in Waterfall is less frequent, while Agile includes frequent feedback loops.
- Waterfall usually has heavier documentation; Agile documentation is lighter but still purposeful.

## Part 3: Walk Through Comparison Table 
Key differences to highlight:
- **Flexibility:** Agile, Scrum, Kanban, and DevOps are generally more adaptive than Waterfall.
- **Documentation:** Waterfall and Spiral usually demand more formal documents than Kanban.
- **Project fit:** DevOps is strong for continuous delivery; Spiral is strong for high-risk projects.

## Part 4: Preferred Model for Student Team Project 
I would choose **Scrum** for a student team project.
Scrum gives a clear structure through sprint planning, daily stand-ups, and sprint reviews.
This structure helps students divide work clearly, track progress weekly, and improve communication.
It is flexible enough to handle requirement changes during the semester.
